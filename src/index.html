How musically similar top songs are?

Our charts are getting sadder, but almost equally danceable.

By Ruobing Su

The year of 2018 has been a pretty productive year for Drake - precisely, since the year of 2010, Drake has been one of the most popular hip pop singer on Spotify who owns over 35 million followers. His track, More Life, became Spotify’s most popular opening day album ever, with more than 60 million streams in the first 24 hours. Amazingly, his 2018 Scorpion album broke the record for a second time; it was listened to more than 130 million times in the first 24 hours after release.

Singers like Drake who have predominant reputation on hip-pop, such as Jay-Z, Kanye West and Rihanna, have been in the charts for more than a decade since debut. Many research and studies show that now-a-days, hit songs are becoming less varied musically, especially when rap music becomes today’s pop music.

Is there something unique about today’s hits—artists taking fewer risks and creating a narrower range of sounds?

Using the data set extracted from Spotify’s web API, I exam how pop music diverse or change over the year in terms of audio features by visualizing the hot tracks of each year’s hot 100 tracks.

Wait, who are those pop stars on hot 100 tracks over the year?
